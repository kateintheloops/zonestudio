case studies

competitive intelligence
Mapping the market before building the product
B2B SaaS · Early-stage founder · Pre-launch
the situation

A solo founder was three months into building a project management tool for creative agencies. She had a rough sense of the competitive landscape but was making pricing and positioning decisions based on vibes — a few competitor websites she'd bookmarked, nothing structured.

what we did
  • Mapped 11 direct and adjacent competitors across pricing, features, and messaging
  • Pulled and analysed 200+ app store and G2 reviews to surface what users actually complained about
  • Identified the specific language customers used to describe their frustrations
  • Benchmarked feature sets to find gaps no one was addressing
what we found
  • The $49–$79/mo tier was completely uncontested — everyone priced either below $30 or above $100
  • The top complaint across all competitors: onboarding took too long, users churned in week one
  • One competitor had just raised and was visibly shifting upmarket — leaving SMB customers underserved
The outcome: She launched at $59/mo with onboarding as her lead differentiator — positioning built entirely around the gap the research revealed, not a guess.
internal data strategy
Turning two years of user data into a growth roadmap
B2B Platform · Scale-up · Post-product-market fit
the situation

A scale-up had been collecting user data for two years — usage logs, support tickets, churn records, NPS responses. The data existed, but no one had ever built a system to actually read it. The team was making retention and expansion decisions based on quarterly anecdotes from sales calls.

what we did
  • Audited all existing data sources and mapped what was captured vs. what was missing
  • Built a unified view connecting product usage, support history, and revenue data
  • Ran pattern analysis across churned vs. retained accounts to isolate early warning signals
  • Identified the three user behaviours that consistently predicted 90-day churn
what we found
  • Accounts that didn't complete a specific setup step in week one churned at 3× the rate
  • Their highest-value segment was using a feature the team considered secondary
  • Support ticket volume was a lagging indicator — the real signal was login frequency drop-off
The outcome: A prioritised action plan — a targeted onboarding intervention, a repositioned feature, and an early warning dashboard the team could actually use. No new hires required.
